My education

Learning through projects is arguably the best way to learn programming; however, solely learning through projects will leave you without the theoretical basis on which programming knowledge thrives. Indeed, I believe that learning about core topics in mathematics, engineering, and computer science will allow a software engineer to understand why something works or how to make it work, faster, making them better at what they do.

You will find information about my educational curriculum below.

It seems that every time I progress in my education curriculum, I find myself enjoying more the position I'm in. When I went from high school to college/CEGEP, I enjoyed the additional freedom and the more advanced topics I was learning about. And when I got to university, I knew I was on the right track; what was previously a hobby of mine that I used to get away from the stress of school was now the main focus of my studies, and was what professors were teaching me!

In my first semester, I enrolled in the McGill Artificial Intelligence Society's "MAIS 202" bootcamp, an accelerated introduction to AI and ML. You can learn more about my learning experience below.

Thankfully, McGill will be back to full in-person classes and activities this next semester, and I will be able to experience more of the student life.

MAIS 202 Bootcamp

Oct 2021 - Nov 2021
  • Full course load during two months during the Fall 2021 semester.
  • Learned about various machine learning algorithms.
  • Created an AI-based webapp game for the final assignment.

The McGill Artificial Intelligence Society is a student-lead community of AI enthusiasts with the goal of learning and teaching, fostering the AI community, and connecting students with the industry. Their "MAIS 202" bootcamp is a two-month accelerated introduction to AI and ML with assignments and classes worth a full course load.

The final assignment for this bootcamp was to build an AI-based project, that we completed in pairs. The project that I worked on is described in the projects page.
